Tabletop Treasures

Keshaun Warren

I better not see you again,” the guard told his passenger with a wink as he pulled up to the bus stop.

“I never intended to be there to begin with, but you see how well that worked,” the passenger shouldered his pack and, out of habit, waiting for the guard to let him out.

“Oh no,” the guard said, “you’re a free man now. You open your own doors!”

It was an odd sensation, opening a door to the outside of his own free will. He’d all but forgotten how it felt. The passenger slid open the door to the prison transport van and stepped out into the sunlight. For the first time in 15 years, he was free.
